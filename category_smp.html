Present, Past, and Future Continuous Tenses

Past Continuous Tense:
• Describes actions or events that were ongoing in the past.
• Formed using "was" (for singular subjects) or "were" (for plural subjects) + the base form of the verb + "-ing."
• Used to describe something that was happening at a specific time in the past.

Future Continuous Tense:
• Describes actions or events that will be ongoing in the future.
• Formed using "will be" + the base form of the verb + "-ing.

Present Continuous Tense:
• Describes actions or events that are currently ongoing in the present.
• Formed using "am" (for the first person singular), "is" (for the third person singular), "are" (for plural subjects) + the base form of the verb + "-ing."

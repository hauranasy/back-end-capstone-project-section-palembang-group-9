Narrative Text:

A narrative text is a form of storytelling that presents a sequence of events or a story to entertain, inform, or engage the reader. It typically follows a structure that includes characters, setting, plot, and a theme or message. Here's an explanation of the key elements of the narrative text provided:

1. Title
This is the title of the story, which often gives readers a hint about the theme or focus of the narrative.
2. Introduction
The narrative begins by introducing the main character, Ella, and her background. It also establishes the setting, a village near a mysterious forest, and hints at Ella's curiosity and love for nature.
3. Conflict/Inciting Incident
The narrative introduces the central conflict or inciting incident when Ella decides to explore the enchanted forest on her own. This decision sets the story in motion.
4. Character Development
As Ella ventures into the forest, the narrative provides insight into her character, describing her emotions, thoughts, and reactions to the forest's beauty and mysteries.
